# Quickstart guide

A short tour of what's in Project Tracker and where to find it. Everything
lives in the menu bar at the top (or the sidebar on the left).

## Projects & Tasks
Start here. Add a **Project** for each business or area you're running, then
add **Tasks** under it. Tasks can be scheduled by day and viewed as a table,
a board, or a calendar — switch views from the Tasks page. Log time against
a task with a **Work session**; it rolls up into your weekly totals
automatically.

## CRM
Track people you're talking to — leads, potential clients, anyone you're
following up with. Each contact has a status (New → Contacted → Replied →
In conversation → Converted), a place to log interactions (DMs, calls,
emails), and up to three follow-up stages with reminders. The **Follow-ups**
page shows everyone who's due.

## Assets
This is where you keep raw material you might want to turn into content:
transcripts, testimonials, old posts, notes. **You'll see two example
assets already loaded** ("[Example] Client testimonial transcript" and
"[Example] Past blog post") — open one to see what a real asset looks like,
and delete them (there's a Delete button on each) once you've added your
own.

From any asset, you can **Generate content** — pick a prompt template (blog
post, social caption, email, funnel copy, or documentation) and Project
Tracker will turn that asset into a draft, saved as a new Page you can edit.
**This needs an AI key to work** — add one under **⚙️ Settings**, see
`SETTING-UP-AI-FEATURES.md`. Until you add one, you'll see a clear message
instead of an error.

### Voice profiles
Under Assets → Voice profiles, you can teach the AI to write in your voice.
Pick a few assets that represent how you actually write, save a voice
profile, then click **Distill from sources** — it reads those samples and
fills in tone, sentence length, and things to avoid, which then get used
automatically whenever you generate content with that voice selected.
**There's an example voice profile already loaded** ("[Example] Warm &
Direct") so you can see what a finished one looks like before making your
own.

## Products
Track what you sell. Add a **Product** (name, price, type, status), log
**Sales** against it as they happen, and see your total revenue per
product. You can also set up **Funnel steps** (order bumps, upsells) per
product from the admin area, linked to the product page.

## Pages
Notion-style notes. Create a page, link it to a Project or a Contact, and
write freely — headers, lists, checklists, tables. Anything generated by
the Assets feature is also saved here as a new page, so your AI drafts and
your own notes live in the same place.

## Removing the example content
Everything pre-loaded is labeled **"[Example]"** so it's easy to spot:

- **Assets** — open each example asset and click Delete.
- **Voice profile** — open it and delete it from the admin area
  (Admin → Assets → Voice profiles).
- **Prompt templates** — these are genuinely useful as-is (they're generic
  templates, not fake data) — you can leave them, edit them, or delete them
  from Admin → Assets → Prompt templates.
- **Example page** — find it under Pages and move it to Trash.

## Where's my data?
Everything is stored locally on your own computer — nothing is sent to a
server (except the direct AI calls you choose to make, if you set up an AI
key). Your data lives in a folder Windows manages for you automatically; you
don't need to think about it unless you want to back it up.
